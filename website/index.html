Common Questions

What happens if I sell my house?
Solar panels add an average of $20,000 to your home's value in NJ, and the state exempts that increase from property tax. Homes with solar sell 17% faster. The panels and warranty transfer to the new owner.
Is there really no upfront cost?
With a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) or solar lease, there is no upfront cost. The solar company installs and maintains the panels, and you pay them for the electricity at a rate lower than your utility. This involves a long-term agreement, typically 20 to 25 years. The consultation itself is completely free with no obligation. If solar doesn't make sense for your home, we'll tell you.
How long does installation take?
The consultation takes about 30 minutes. If you decide to move forward, permits, installation, and utility connection usually take 6 to 10 weeks total. The actual rooftop work is done in 1 to 2 days.
What's the catch?
The solar company pays for installation because they make money selling you electricity at a rate lower than the utility. You pay nothing unless you decide to go solar, and the consultation itself is always free with no obligation.
