## EDUCATION POSTS (4)

---

### Post 1 — NJ Incentive Breakdown

**NJ has some of the best solar incentives in the country — here's the quick breakdown:**

1. **30% federal tax credit** — dollar-for-dollar off your tax bill, not just a deduction
2. **NJ SRECs** — the state pays you for every megawatt-hour your panels produce, and your panels generate several certificates per year
3. **Property tax exemption** — solar can add up to $20K to home value, but NJ won't increase your property tax for it
4. **Net metering** — excess power you generate gets credited to your bill

Most of these have expiration dates. Happy to answer questions if anyone's curious — I help neighbors in the area go solar and I genuinely love talking about this stuff.

---

### Post 2 — How Solar Actually Works (Demystified)

**Had a neighbor ask me last week: "But what happens when it's cloudy?"**

Fair question. Here's the simple version:

Your panels convert sunlight to electricity. On cloudy days, they still produce — just less (about 25-40% of peak). At night, you pull from the grid like normal.

But here's the key: NJ has net metering. So all that extra power you make on sunny days? It gets banked as credits. You use those credits at night and in winter.

Many NJ solar homeowners I work with have a near-zero electric bill — year round. That's the true bill, not a cherry-picked summer month.

Ask me anything. No agenda, just like talking about this.

---

### Post 3 — Rate Hike Explainer

**PSE&G just got approved for another rate increase.**

If it feels like your electric bill goes up every year... it's because it does. NJ rates have climbed 47% over the past decade. The utility files for an increase, the BPU approves most of it, and your bill goes up.

Solar doesn't fix everything. But it does one powerful thing: it locks your electricity cost at $0 for 25+ years. The sun doesn't send rate increase notices.

If you've been on the fence, this might be worth a second look. Happy to run numbers for anyone — free consultation, takes about 5 minutes.

---

### Post 4 — The "Is My Roof Good Enough?" Post

**"I'd go solar but my roof faces the wrong way."**

I hear this a lot. Here's the truth: south-facing is ideal, but east and west-facing roofs work great in NJ too. The production difference is usually only 10-15%.

What actually matters more:
- Shade (big trees directly over your roof)
- Roof age (if you need a new roof in 5 years, do that first)
- Available space (you need about 300 sq ft for a decent system)

North-facing is the only direction that's genuinely tough. Everything else? Probably works.

If you're curious about your specific house, I can check it in about 2 minutes using satellite imagery. Just shoot me a message.
