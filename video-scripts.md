## GENERAL FILMING AND STRATEGY GUIDE

### Filming Tips

- **Lighting:** Natural light is your best friend. Film facing a window or outside during golden hour (the hour after sunrise or before sunset). Never film with a window behind you -- your face will be dark. If filming indoors at night, get a cheap ring light ($20 on Amazon) and place it directly in front of you at eye level.
- **Background:** Keep it clean and intentional. Best options: in front of a house with solar panels, standing next to your truck/van, a clean home office setup, or outside in a NJ neighborhood. Avoid clutter. A branded hat or polo adds credibility without being salesy.
- **Audio:** Film in quiet spaces. Wind is the enemy outdoors -- use your phone's wired earbuds as a mic if it is windy. Speak clearly and slightly louder than normal conversation.
- **Energy Level:** Whatever energy you think is enough, add 30% more. The camera flattens energy. Practice your hook three times before recording. Stand up when filming -- it adds energy to your voice even if you are only visible from the chest up.
- **Framing:** Vertical (9:16) always. Eyes in the upper third of the frame. Leave room at the top and bottom for platform UI and captions.
- **Length:** Aim for 35-50 seconds. Under 60 seconds is mandatory. Shorter is almost always better -- if you can say it in 30, do not stretch to 45.
- **Takes:** Film each script 3-5 times. Your best take is almost never the first one.

### Best Posting Times for a New Jersey Audience

- **Weekdays:** 7:00-8:00 AM (morning commute scroll), 12:00-1:00 PM (lunch break), 7:00-9:00 PM (evening wind-down)
- **Weekends:** 9:00-11:00 AM, 7:00-9:00 PM
- **Best days overall:** Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday
- **Post consistently.** Three videos per week minimum. Same days each week so your audience expects you.

### Hashtag Strategy

Use a mix of three tiers every time:

1. **High volume (discovery):** #solar #solarenergy #renewableenergy #solarpanels -- these get your video into broad feeds
2. **Mid volume (targeted):** #njsolar #newjerseysolar #solarsavings #energybills #homeowner -- these reach people actually interested
3. **Low volume (local/niche):** #pseg #njhomeowner #jerseysolar #njenergy #solarnj -- these have less competition, so you rank higher

Rotate your hashtags across videos. Do not copy-paste the same set every time -- the algorithm deprioritizes that.

### Repurposing Across Platforms

Every video you film works on all three platforms with minor adjustments:

- **TikTok:** Post the raw, authentic version first. TikTok rewards less polished content. Use TikTok's native captions and trending sounds (low volume under your voice). Add 1-2 TikTok-specific hashtags like #LearnOnTikTok or #TikTokPartner.
- **Instagram Reels:** Post the same video but add a polished cover image with a bold text title. Write a longer caption (3-5 sentences expanding on the topic, ending with a question to drive comments). Use Instagram's native caption tool. Share to your Story with a "New Reel" sticker.
- **YouTube Shorts:** Upload the same video. Write a keyword-rich title (YouTube is a search engine). Add a 2-3 sentence description with your main keyword in the first line. Pin a comment with your CTA and link.

**Posting order:** TikTok first (same day) --> Reels next day --> Shorts the day after. This gives each platform fresh engagement windows and avoids the algorithm penalizing cross-posted content detected at the same timestamp.

**Platform:** TikTok / Reels / Shorts

**Script:**

Your electric meter can actually spin backward. And when it does, PSE and G owes you. Let me explain.

During the day when the sun is shining, your solar panels are producing electricity. Your home uses some of it for whatever is running -- your fridge, your AC, your lights. But here is the thing. On a sunny day, your panels usually produce more than your home needs.

That extra energy does not just disappear. It flows out of your home and into the grid. And when that happens, your meter literally runs in reverse. PSE and G tracks every kilowatt hour you send back and gives you a credit on your bill.

Then at night when the sun is down and your panels are not producing, you pull electricity from the grid like normal. But instead of paying for it, you use those credits you banked during the day.

Think of it like this. During the day you are making deposits. At night you are making withdrawals. At the end of the month, most solar homeowners I work with are close to net zero -- meaning their bill is basically just the small connection fee.

This is called net metering and it is one of the biggest reasons solar works so well in New Jersey.

## WHAT TO DO AFTER POSTING

1. **Respond to every comment within the first hour.** The algorithm boosts videos with early engagement. Even a simple reply counts.
2. **Pin your best comment.** Write a comment on your own video expanding on one point or asking a question. Pin it to encourage replies.
3. **Share to Stories.** Every time you post a Reel, share it to your Instagram Story with a "New video" text overlay and a poll sticker ("Did you know this? Yes/No").
4. **Track what works.** After 4 weeks, look at which videos got the most saves (not views -- saves indicate purchase intent). Make more content like your top 3.
5. **Batch film.** Pick one day per week. Film all 3 videos back to back. Change your shirt between takes so they look like different days.
