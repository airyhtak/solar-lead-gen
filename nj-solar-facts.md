# NJ Solar Facts — Use in Ads, Content, and Conversations

## NJ SREC-II Income (THE BEST HOOK)
- NJ requires utilities to buy Solar Renewable Energy Certificates from solar owners
- Current rate: ~$90 per MWh
- Typical 10 kW system produces ~12 MWh/year = **~$1,080/year in SREC income**
- This is PASSIVE INCOME on top of electric savings
- Qualification period: 15 years from activation
- Hook: "NJ literally pays you to have solar panels"

## Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC)
- **30% tax credit** on total system cost through 2032
- On a $25,000 system: **$7,500 credit**
- Steps down to 26% in 2033, 22% in 2034
- Hook: "The federal government covers 30% of the cost"

## NJ Property Tax Exemption
- **100% property tax exemption** on added home value from solar
- Solar adds ~4% to home value (Zillow research)
- On a $400K home: $16K in added value, ZERO additional property taxes
- NJ has some of the HIGHEST property taxes in the country — this resonates
- Hook: "Your home is worth more but your taxes stay the same"

## NJ Sales Tax Exemption
- Solar equipment exempt from NJ's 6.625% sales tax
- On a $25,000 system: **$1,656 saved**

## NJ Net Metering
- Excess solar energy credits roll over month to month
- Credits valued at **full retail rate** (not wholesale — this is huge)
- Annual true-up in April
- Hook: "Overproduce in summer, use credits in winter = low bills year-round"

## Utility Rate Increases (PAIN POINT)
- PSE&G rates up ~40-50% over the last decade
- JCP&L similar trajectory
- Average NJ residential rate: $0.18-0.22/kWh (among highest in US)
- Solar equivalent: ~$0.06-0.10/kWh over 25 years
- Hook: "Your bill only goes up. Solar freezes it."

## Typical NJ System Economics
- Average system: 8-10 kW
- Cost before incentives: $20,000-28,000
- Cost after ITC: $14,000-20,000
- Monthly savings: $100-250 depending on usage
- Payback period: typically 5-8 years
- System lifespan: 25-30 years
- After payback: 17-22 years of essentially free electricity + SREC income

## 5 Qualifying Questions (Use Everywhere)

1. **"Do you own your home?"** — Renters can't install. Eliminates 30%+.
2. **"How old is your roof?"** — Over 20 years = yellow flag. May need roof first.
3. **"Average monthly electric bill?"** — Under $80 = weak ROI. Sweet spot: $150-400. Over $400 = high priority.
4. **"What's your utility?"** — PSE&G, JCP&L, Atlantic City Electric all have slightly different programs.
5. **"Timeline?"** — "Just looking" vs "next 3 months" determines follow-up urgency.
